<p class="TEXT">
              Congratulations to Roger Wellington-Oguri, Eli Fisker, Mathew Zada, Michelle Wiley, Jill Townley, and Eterna Players for the <b>first peer-reviewed scientific publication written entirely by videogame players</b>. The paper <a href="https://dx.doi.org/10.1021/acs.biochem.0c00215">"Evidence of an Unusual Poly(A) RNA Signature Detected by High-throughput Chemical Mapping" </a> was just accepted in the journal <i>Biochemistry</i>. The paper is coming out back to back with a <a href="https://dx.doi.org/10.1021/acs.biochem.0c00020">companion paper</a> from our lab that validates the players' discovery of an unusual polyadenosine signature in RNA structure mapping experiments. These same <b>player authors</b> are leading scientific discussions in Eterna's <a href="https://challenges.eternagame.org">OpenVaccine mRNA vaccine stabilization</a> efforts.
            </p>
